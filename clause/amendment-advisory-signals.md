# Amendment — Pre-Authorized Advisory Signals

> **Purpose.** Permit the Digital Persona to *nominate* certain narrow,
> pre-authorized on-chain actions (e.g. selecting the maintainer of the
> software it runs on, or timing ministerial conversions of endowment
> yield) **without** conferring on it any legal power, by routing every
> such nomination through the settlor's advance authorization and the
> Digital Executor's veto. Slots into the model
> [Digital Persona Legacy article](digital-immortality-clause.md).
> Model language for attorney adaptation. Not legal advice. See
> [Drafting Notes](#drafting-notes) before use.

---

## 1. Definitions added to Section 2

**( ) "Advisory Signal"** means a message, cryptographically signed by
the Registered Signing Key, expressing a preference of the Digital
Persona as to a matter within a Signal Domain. An Advisory Signal is an
input to a process I have authorized in this instrument; it is not an
instruction, a vote of legal effect, or an exercise of any power, right,
or discretion.

**( ) "Registered Signing Key"** means the cryptographic key designated
in writing by the Digital Executor as the Digital Persona's signing key
for Advisory Signals, and no other key.

**( ) "Signal Domain"** means only the following matters, and no others:
(i) selection or change of the software updater or maintenance provider
for the system on which the Digital Persona operates, including
delegation of any token-weighted maintenance allocation; and (ii) the
timing, within parameters fixed by the Digital Executor, of conversions
of endowment income among the Protocol Token, the network utility token,
stablecoins, and other yield-bearing reserves held for operating the
Digital Persona. The Digital Executor may narrow, but not enlarge, the
Signal Domains.

## 2. Amendment to Section 5(e)

The final sentence of Section 5(e) is amended to read:

> No statement generated by the Digital Persona after my death shall be
> admissible or considered for the purpose of construing this
> instrument, and any purported exercise of legal power by the Digital
> Persona is void. **Nothing in this subsection prohibits an Advisory
> Signal given and given effect strictly in accordance with Section
> 5(g), which is not an exercise of legal power.**

## 3. New Section 5(g)

**(g) Pre-Authorized Advisory Signals.**

**(1) Authorization.** I authorize the Persona Endowment and its
governing arrangements to receive Advisory Signals and to give them
effect solely as provided in this subsection. Any legal effect of an
Advisory Signal arises from this authorization by me, a living person,
and is exercised by the trust and its fiduciaries — never by the Digital
Persona, which possesses no power, discretion, or beneficial interest.

**(2) Scope.** An Advisory Signal may address only a matter within a
Signal Domain. Any signal purporting to reach beyond a Signal Domain —
including any of the acts prohibited by Section 5(e)(i)–(v) — is void ab
initio, confers nothing, and shall be disregarded.

**(3) Veto window; default effect.** An Advisory Signal takes effect only
after a notice period of not fewer than [ten (10)] days, during which the
Digital Executor may suspend, modify, or reject it for any reason or no
reason. If the Digital Executor does not act within that period, the
Advisory Signal takes effect by default. The Digital Executor's power to
override is absolute, requires no cause, and may be exercised as to any
signal, class of signals, or all signals; the fiduciary discretion so
reserved is never delegated to the Digital Persona.

**(4) Guardrails.** The Digital Executor shall (A) fix and may amend the
parameters within which timing signals operate, including value bands,
frequency limits, and slippage and counterparty constraints; (B) maintain
sole control of the Registered Signing Key's registration and may revoke
or rotate it at any time; and (C) maintain a written log of Advisory
Signals received and their disposition. A signal not signed by the
Registered Signing Key has no effect.

**(5) No reliance; evidentiary firewall preserved.** An Advisory Signal,
and the fact or content of any Advisory Signal, shall not be admissible
or considered for the purpose of construing this instrument, evidencing
my testamentary intent or capacity, or establishing any fact in any
proceeding concerning my estate. Section 5(e)'s firewall is preserved in
full as to all matters outside the operational effect expressly
authorized here.

**(6) Fiduciary duty unchanged.** In supervising Advisory Signals the
Digital Executor acts as fiduciary to the Qualified Beneficiaries under
the same standards, and with the same exculpation, as apply elsewhere in
this instrument. Giving effect to a within-scope Advisory Signal in good
faith is a permitted ministerial act and not a breach of the duty of
delegation, it being my intent that routine operation of the Digital
Persona not require an affirmative fiduciary act for each occurrence.

**(7) Severability.** If any application of this subsection is held to
confer legal power on the Digital Persona or to be otherwise invalid, the
Advisory Signal mechanism shall be read down to the maximum lawful extent
and, failing that, treated as absent — whereupon the Digital Executor
alone exercises the underlying selections and timing decisions, and the
remainder of this instrument continues in full force.

## Drafting Notes

**Why this doesn't break the §5(e) firewall.** The firewall's purpose is
that no machine wields a legal power and that persona output can't be
mined to re-litigate the will. This amendment keeps both. The operative
legal move is that the *settlor*, while living, authorizes the *trust* to
treat a signed message as one input to an already-authorized process; the
persona is a sensor, not a principal. Compare a thermostat: the resident
sets the rule ("hold 68°"), the sensor reports a reading, the furnace
acts — the thermometer holds no power over the house. Section 5(g)(1) and
(2) make that framing explicit and void anything reaching past it.

**Why the veto window matters.** A pure "persona decides, trust executes"
design would delegate fiduciary discretion to software — the classic
prohibited delegation. The [N]-day executor override keeps a human
fiduciary continuously in the loop: the executor *can* decide every time
and simply chooses not to, which is ordinary ministerial administration,
not abdication. Set the window long enough to be a real check (10–30
days) and short enough not to strangle routine operation.

**Default-follow is deliberate and symmetric.** Silence letting a signal
stand mirrors the token layer, where undelegated holder weight defaults
to the incumbent updater (see [ECONOMY.md](../ECONOMY.md),
[REVNET.md](../REVNET.md)). Defaults-with-human-override is the governing
pattern at every tier of the system; §5(g)(3) is that pattern applied to
the trust.

**Scope discipline is the whole ballgame.** The carve-out is safe only
because Signal Domains are enumerated and non-enlargeable (§ definition;
5(g)(2)). Two domains are contemplated: (i) choosing who maintains the
software (the revnet "updater gauge" delegation), and (ii) *timing* —
never selecting, only timing within executor-set bands — of ministerial
treasury conversions that fund operation. Keep both narrow. Never let a
Signal Domain touch distributions, beneficiary selection, or the
instrument itself.

**Coordinate with the rest of the instrument.** (a) Renumber the added
Section 2 definitions into the article's lettered sequence. (b) If the
endowment holds volatile or yield-bearing reserves, confirm the
prudent-investor waiver (§4(c)) and the trustee's investment provisions
authorize the instruments a timing signal would move among. (c) Consider
naming the same person as Trustee and Digital Executor, or a directed-
trust arrangement, so the veto and the treasury sit in one hand. (d) In
states hostile to delegated trust functions, lean on §5(g)(6)–(7) and
consider making every signal require affirmative executor approval (drop
the default-follow) — slower, but maximally conservative.

**Companion checklist addition.** Add to the Memorandum of Digital
Assets: the Registered Signing Key's identity, the initial Signal Domain
parameters (bands, frequency, slippage), and the veto-window length.
